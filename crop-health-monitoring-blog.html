The Power of Remote Sensing for Crop Health

Traditional methods of assessing crop health often involve manual scouting, which can be time-consuming, labor-intensive, and prone to inaccuracies. Remote sensing offers a more efficient and precise approach. By capturing data from above, we can gain a comprehensive view of your fields and detect subtle changes in plant health that might be missed by the naked eye.

Geomad utilizes advanced sensors mounted on satellites and drones to capture images across various wavelengths of light, including visible and near-infrared regions. This data is then processed using sophisticated algorithms to generate valuable insights into your crop's condition.

Key Spectral Indices: NDVI and NDRE

Two essential tools in our crop health monitoring arsenal are the Normalized Difference Vegetation Index (NDVI) and the Normalized Difference Red Edge (NDRE). These indices are calculated from the reflectance values of specific wavelengths and provide valuable information about vegetation health and density.

  • NDVI (Normalized Difference Vegetation Index)

    NDVI is a widely used index that measures the difference between near-infrared (NIR) and red light reflectance. Healthy vegetation absorbs more red light and reflects more NIR light, resulting in higher NDVI values. NDVI is particularly useful for:

    • Assessing overall vegetation health and vigor
    • Monitoring vegetation growth stages
    • Estimating biomass and yield potential
    • Detecting early signs of stress, such as drought or nutrient deficiencies

  • NDRE (Normalized Difference Red Edge)

    NDRE is similar to NDVI but utilizes the red edge band instead of the red band. The red edge band is more sensitive to chlorophyll content, making NDRE particularly effective for:

    • Detecting subtle changes in chlorophyll content
    • Monitoring nitrogen levels in plants
    • Assessing crop health during later growth stages
    • Detecting stress in crops with dense canopies

By analyzing NDVI and NDRE values over time, we can identify areas of concern within your fields, allowing for targeted interventions and optimized resource allocation.
